## Core structure of the source post

The post groups events into four buckets:

1. **Robotics / Physical AI**
   - Signals: embodied intelligence, robotics systems, hardware-software integration

2. **Large Model / Agent ecosystem**
   - Signals: multimodal applications, agent demos, LLM infra, search/recommendation with LLMs

3. **VC / Pitch circles**
   - Signals: founder-investor matching, Demo Days, capital networking

4. **“Unusual” social events**
   - Signals: low-formality environments where cross-domain trust builds faster

This is a practical funnel:
**tech signal collection → product opportunity validation → capital/collab matching → weak-tie expansion.**

## 6 practical builder takeaways

## 1) Don’t optimize only for the main conference

GTC main sessions are usually broadcast channels; side events are relationship channels.

**Execution rule:**
- 1 main session + 1–2 side events per day
- prioritize events with clear participant profiles
- log people, pain points, and explicit next steps

## 2) Physical AI is moving from “research prestige” to “deployment conversations”

The source gives robotics/physical AI first placement and strong density. That’s a market signal: teams are shifting from pure model talk to system-level deliverability.

**Execution rule:**
- software teams: prepare a story for hardware/sensor/control integration
- robotics teams: present your data loop + model iteration + safety constraints

## 3) “Model → Agent → Infrastructure” is the dominant narrative

Multiple listed events explicitly center on agentic AI and supporting infra.

**Execution rule:**
- bring a one-page architecture: model layer, agent runtime, tooling, data plane
- demo 2–3 real tasks (not benchmark theater)
- be ready with reliability/cost/latency numbers

## 4) Investor events are pipeline starts, not end states

Pitch events don’t close rounds instantly. They seed high-quality follow-on threads.

**Execution rule:**
- segment investors by thesis-stage-geo fit
- ask one sharp question per conversation
- send follow-up within 24 hours with a concrete next step

## 5) Informal events are trust accelerators

The source’s “unusual events” section is strategically smart: many high-value connections happen outside formal panels.

**Execution rule:**
- reserve ~20% of schedule for low-pressure social formats
- optimize for alignment discovery, not hard selling

## 6) Geographic planning is a hard constraint

Event spread across Bay Area cities means transit can kill your ROI.

**Execution rule:**
- cluster your day by city
- keep 90 minutes buffer daily
- set minimum output targets (e.g., 3 quality new contacts + 1 concrete follow-up)

## Reusable operating template for GTC week

## Pre-event (T-7 to T-1)
- shortlist 8–12 events by topic + location
- prep: 30s intro, 2min product narrative, 5min demo
- pre-build contact tracking sheet

## During event (daily)
- daytime: technical signal collection
- evening: network expansion
- end-of-day: unified follow-up dispatch

## Post-event (T+1 to T+14)
- first follow-up within 48h
- second touchpoint within 7 days (specific collaboration hook)
- synthesize a “theme map” from repeated market questions

---

## Limits of the source (and what builders must add)

The source is excellent as a **navigation layer**, but not an event quality audit.

You still need to add:

1. **Event quality filtering** (host track record, attendee quality, agenda clarity)
2. **ROI estimation** (time + transit + opportunity cost)
3. **Goal binding** (each event tied to hiring, customer, capital, or partnership outcomes)
