## The 9-Module Framework

This prompt structure is worth studying for anyone serious about AI image generation. It's not keyword soup — it's engineered prompt architecture:

### 1. Subject Definition
- Base character info: young woman, refined features, Korean actress-style makeup
- Critical framing: "late night bedroom casual selfie" — this single phrase sets the entire mood

### 2. Expression & Facial Detail
- Independent control over eye direction, mouth curvature, overall expression
- "Sweet but restrained" is far more precise than "smiling"
- Explicit instruction to preserve facial asymmetry and natural proportions — fighting AI's tendency toward perfect faces

### 3. Hair Setup
- Color, state, and strand effects specified separately
- "Slightly messy" beats "neat long hair" for scene authenticity

### 4. Body & Skin Texture
- The longest and most nuanced section
- Skin texture emphasizes "real touchable feel" and explicitly rejects "plastic airbrushed look"
- Flash highlights and shadow falloff are precisely described
- Skin tone uses "fair with warm-neutral undertones" instead of simply "white skin"

### 5. Pose & Composition
- Lying on bed, holding phone, legs raised high
- "Deliberately unbalanced but playful composition" — this is a core aesthetic judgment call
- "Like a casually captured private moment" defines the image's narrative

### 6. Clothing & Props
- Minimalist black loungewear — the focus isn't on clothes but atmosphere
- Phone flash starburst is the key accent detail
- Ribbed knit leg warmers bunched at the ankles — small details that sell realism

### 7. Camera & Shooting Style
- Low angle, wide-angle phone perspective, portrait orientation
- "Phone-quality detail with slight natural grain" — intentionally imperfect
- Depth of field mimics actual phone camera characteristics

### 8. Background & Environment
- Grey walls, plaid bedding, dark pillows, wooden drawer cabinet
- "Private, quiet, slightly lived-in — not too tidy"
- Environmental details carry the entire "real bedroom" believability

### 9. Overall Atmosphere
- Mood keywords: soft, playful, understated, unforced
- "Like the room has gone quiet, capturing a moment in the pause before sleep"
- The defining line: "Casual enough to feel real, beautiful enough to feel distant"

## Why This Prompt Structure Matters

**Modular thinking.** Breaking a single image into 9 independently controllable modules with clear descriptive dimensions. This isn't creative writing — it's prompt engineering.

**Counter-intuitive realism.** Most people write prompts chasing perfection. This one does the opposite — messy bedding, wide-angle distortion, harsh flash, skin grain. These "imperfections" are exactly what makes the output look real.

**Narrative layer.** It doesn't just describe what the image looks like — it describes what the image *feels* like. "A moment captured casually late at night, not a studio shoot, not a posed shot." This narrative-level guidance is advanced prompt technique.

**Chinese-native precision.** Most high-quality AI image prompts are in English. This one proves Chinese-language prompts can achieve equally fine-grained control, which matters as Chinese-language AI models continue to improve.

## Practical Takeaways

1. **Modularize your prompts.** Don't write one giant block — separate subject, expression, clothing, lighting, environment
2. **Add anti-perfection elements.** Grain, messiness, asymmetry — these are the sources of realism
3. **Describe feelings, not just appearances.** "Sweet but restrained smile" outperforms "smiling" every time
4. **Define the scene narrative.** "Late night bedroom selfie" unifies style better than a list of tags
5. **Adopt this 9-module framework.** Even outside AI photography, this structure works for any image generation prompt
