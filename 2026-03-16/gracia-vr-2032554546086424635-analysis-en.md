## TL;DR

Gracia has made 4D Gaussian Splatting (4DGS) videos streamable in the browser — no download, no app. It works on VR headsets, phones, and laptops, with no limits on splat count or video length.

## Why This Matters

Gaussian Splatting has been the hottest technology in 3D/XR for the past two years. It reconstructs real scenes into freely viewable 3D content, far surpassing traditional mesh-based approaches. But there's always been one critical bottleneck: **distribution**.

Previously, viewing Gaussian Splatting content meant downloading massive PLY files, installing dedicated apps, or running it on high-end PCs. It's like watching video in 2005 — cool tech, but inaccessible to normal people.

Gracia's breakthrough is making **4DGS streamable**. Just as YouTube turned video into "click and watch," Gracia is turning volumetric video into "click and watch."

## Technical Breakdown

### What is 4DGS?

4D Gaussian Splatting = 3D Gaussian Splatting + the time dimension. In plain terms: a "freely viewable 3D scene" that now moves and changes over time — essentially a 3D video you can watch from any angle.

### What Gracia Built

Based on the tweet and their website, the key technical achievements:

1. **45x Compression Ratio**: Near-lossless 45x compression compared to naive per-frame PLY training. This is the prerequisite for streaming — without it, the data simply can't be transmitted fast enough.

2. **Adaptive Bitrates**: From 17 Mbps (lightweight delivery) to 75 Mbps (full fidelity). Same concept as traditional video ABR — dynamically adjusting quality based on network conditions.

3. **Infinite Frame Interpolation**: Generate 240 FPS playback from 30 FPS source footage with perfect temporal stability — no jitter or artifacts.

4. **Cross-Platform Rendering Performance**:
   - Quest 3/3s: 72 FPS
   - Vision Pro: 100 FPS (coming soon)
   - Mac (Apple Silicon): 150+ FPS
   - Desktop PC: 500+ FPS

5. **WebXR Support**: Plays directly in the browser via WebXR. No plugins, no apps, no downloads.

### Why This Wasn't Possible Before

Volumetric video data dwarfs traditional 2D video. One minute of raw 4DGS content can be tens of gigabytes. Streaming requires solving three problems simultaneously:

- Extreme compression ratios (Gracia achieved 45x)
- Streaming decoders (progressive playback, not download-then-play)
- Real-time rendering on constrained devices (including mobile VR headsets)

All three needed to be solved together. That's what makes this a genuine breakthrough.
