## Why Do AI Videos Look "Fake"?

Many AI video creators nail composition and camera movement, but the result still feels off. The answer is almost always **lighting and color**. In real filmmaking, 80% of the visual mood comes from how a scene is lit. This guide breaks down cinematic lighting into actionable knowledge for AI prompt writing.

---

## 1. Hard Light vs Soft Light

| Type | Characteristics | Use Cases | AI Prompt Keywords |
|------|----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Hard Light** | Sharp edges, defined shadows, high contrast | Thriller, crime, dramatic conflict | `harsh lighting`, `hard shadows`, `high contrast lighting` |
| **Soft Light** | Gradual transitions, subtle shadows, even | Romance, everyday, portraits | `soft lighting`, `diffused light`, `gentle illumination` |

**Pro tip:** Stop defaulting to `cinematic lighting` — decide whether you want hard or soft light first. That single choice defines the entire mood.

---

## 2. Four Lighting Directions

### Front Light
- Even illumination, minimal shadows
- Best for: news, interviews, beauty
- Prompt: `front lighting`, `flat lighting`
- ⚠️ Can look flat and lack depth

### Side Light
- One side bright, one dark — emphasizes contour and texture
- Best for: portraits, mystery, art films
- Prompt: `side lighting`, `Rembrandt lighting`, `split lighting`
- This is the most "cinematic" direction

### Back Light
- Subject silhouetted or rimmed with light
- Best for: romantic, dreamy, farewell scenes
- Prompt: `backlighting`, `silhouette`, `rim light`, `contre-jour`

### Top Light
- Direct overhead, creates shadows in eye sockets and under chin
- Best for: horror, interrogation, sacred/religious feel
- Prompt: `top lighting`, `overhead light`, `dramatic top-down lighting`

## 3. Color Temperature: The Science of Warm vs Cool

Measured in Kelvin (K):

| Range | Tone | Mood | AI Prompt |
|-------|------|------|-----------|
| 1800-3000K | Warm yellow/orange | Intimate, nostalgic, cozy | `warm color temperature`, `golden hour lighting`, `candlelight warmth` |
| 3500-5000K | Neutral white | Natural, everyday | `neutral lighting`, `natural daylight` |
| 5500-8000K | Cool blue | Tech, isolation, futuristic | `cool color temperature`, `blue-tinted lighting`, `moonlight` |

**Advanced: Warm-cool contrast** — Using both warm and cool sources in one frame creates visual tension. Example: blue moonlight through a window + warm lamp indoors.

Prompt: `warm-cool contrast lighting`, `mixed color temperature`

---

## 4. Three-Point Lighting System

The foundational framework for all cinematic lighting:

```
        [Rim / Back Light]
              ↓
    [Key Light] → Subject ← [Fill Light]
```

| Source | Purpose | Intensity | AI Prompt |
|--------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Key Light** | Defines main light direction and shadow | Strongest | `key light from left`, `dominant light source` |
| **Fill Light** | Softens shadows, controls contrast | Weaker (50-70% of key) | `fill light`, `shadow fill` |
| **Rim Light** | Outlines subject, separates from background | Medium | `rim lighting`, `edge light`, `hair light` |

**Key-to-Fill ratio determines style:**
- 1:1 → Flat, TV/variety show look
- 2:1 → Natural, documentary
- 4:1 → Dramatic, cinematic
- 8:1+ → Film noir, extreme contrast

## 6. Common Beginner Mistakes

1. **Inconsistent light direction** — Light coming from contradictory angles in the same scene
2. **Over-relying on `cinematic lighting`** — Too vague; the AI doesn't know what you actually want
3. **Ignoring shadows** — Shadows are half of lighting; no good shadows = no good light
4. **Random color temperature** — Keep it consistent unless intentionally creating warm-cool contrast
5. **No logical light source** — The frame needs something to explain where the light comes from (windows, lamps, etc.)

## The Framework

Lighting isn't mystical. It's a system with clear rules:

1. **Choose texture** (hard/soft) → 2. **Pick direction** (front/side/back/top) → 3. **Set color temp** (warm/cool) → 4. **Build three-point** (key/fill/rim)

Internalize this flow, and your AI prompts will evolve from "add cinematic lighting" to precisely controlling every beam's direction, intensity, and color.
